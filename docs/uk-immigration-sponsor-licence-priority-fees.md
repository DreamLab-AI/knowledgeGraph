- ### About UK Immigration Sponsor Licence Priority Fees

### Primary Definition

In the context of UK immigration, priority fees refer to additional charges levied by the UK Home Office to expedite the processing of sponsor licence applications and sponsor management requests. These fees enable faster decision times for employers seeking to sponsor skilled workers and other migrants under UK immigration routes.


### Academic Context

The concept of priority fees in immigration and sponsorship contexts is grounded in administrative law and public policy studies, balancing efficient service delivery with resource allocation. Academic foundations include studies on immigration policy efficiency, administrative fee structures, and their socio-economic impacts on migrants and sponsors.

Key research areas include:

- Immigration policy efficiency and processing time optimization
- Administrative fee structures and their impact on sponsor behavior
- Socio-economic impacts on migrants and sponsoring organizations
- Balance between expedited processing and fairness in immigration systems
- Risk of creating two-tier systems favoring wealthier sponsors


### Current Landscape (2025) [Updated 2025]

The UK Home Office has increased priority service fees related to sponsor licence applications and management as of October 21, 2025. These fee changes represent substantial increases in the cost of expedited sponsorship-related services.

#### Priority Sponsor Licence Application Request

The priority fee for new sponsor licence applications rose from £500 to £750—a 50% increase. This service allows applicants to receive faster decisions, typically within 10 working days versus the standard 8 weeks processing time. The service is subject to daily availability and capped slots allocated on a first-come, first-served basis.


#### Post-Licence Priority Service

The priority service fee for existing sponsors seeking expedited changes via the Sponsor Management System increased from £200 to £350—a 75% increase. This service applies to requests such as:

- Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) allocation increases
- Key personnel changes
- Changes to sponsor licence details
- Other sponsor management requests

The post-licence priority service expedites processing to around 5 working days instead of the standard processing time of up to 18 weeks. The service is also subject to daily slot limits (typically 100 per day, available weekdays 7:00-17:00).

### Context and Impact on Employers

The fee increases represent substantial rises in the cost of expedited sponsorship-related services and directly affect employer planning and budgeting for immigration-related processes, especially when timing is critical (e.g., visa expiry or recruitment deadlines).

#### Key Impacts

- Priority services are optional but frequently necessary for urgent or business-critical recruitment needs and management changes
- The maximum number of daily priority slots is limited, heightening competition for access
- The Home Office discontinued its Premium Customer Service for sponsors, which provided dedicated account management for an annual fee (£25,000 for large organizations, £8,000 for small/charitable sponsors)
- These increases reflect policy shifts and cost recovery needs by the Home Office


### Restrictions on Passing Fees to Sponsored Workers [Updated 2025]

The UK Home Office has clarified and expanded restrictions on which sponsorship and immigration costs can be recouped from sponsored workers. These rules, implemented and clarified in April 2025, aim to protect migrant workers from bearing employer-related sponsorship costs.

#### Costs Sponsors Are Prohibited From Recouping

Employers are now expressly prohibited from passing on the following fees to sponsored workers:

- **Sponsor licence fees** (for obtaining or maintaining a sponsor licence)
- **Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) fees**
- **Immigration Skills Charge**
- **Legal fees associated with assigning a CoS or obtaining/maintaining the sponsor licence**
- **Priority service fees for sponsor licence applications** (the £750 fee cannot be passed to workers)

The prohibition on passing on legal fees relating to assigning a Skilled Worker CoS dates back to December 31, 2024. These prohibitions apply across all sponsored workers and violation can result in licence suspension or revocation.


#### Costs That May Be Recouped (With Restrictions)

Employers may recoup certain costs from Skilled Workers, but with important caveats:

- **Visa application fees**
- **Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)**
- **Priority or expedited processing fees** (only when the worker had a genuine choice in using such service)
- **Legal fees related to the visa application** (only when the choice to use services was genuinely the worker's)
- **Dependent application fees**

However, if a sponsor pays such costs upfront and then recoups them from the worker, these amounts are considered "business, immigration or investment costs." They **reduce the worker's salary used in assessing the Minimum Salary Level (MSL)** for immigration compliance purposes. The calculation of salary for this purpose will average deductions over the duration of the visa.


#### Implementation and Compliance Requirements

- The updated rule on salary calculations applies to Skilled Worker visa applications submitted from April 9, 2025, onward
- Sponsors must ensure recouped amounts are considered in salary calculations to avoid falling below the minimum threshold
- Non-compliance risks refusal or curtailment of visas and adverse action against the sponsor's licence
- Only "reasonable and proportionate" costs may be recouped
- There must be a clear contractual agreement with the worker
- Recoupment of priority fees is only permissible if the worker made a genuine choice regarding their use


### Technical Infrastructure

The UK Home Office's digital Sponsor Management System (SMS) facilitates priority requests through online interfaces. Key features include:

- Real-time availability checking for priority slots
- First-come, first-served allocation system
- Limited daily capacity (approximately 100 slots per day for post-licence requests)
- Weekday-only availability (typically 7:00-17:00 GMT)
- Integrated payment processing
- Status tracking and notifications
